# AXIS Content Voice Guide

> This is the human-readable version of `VOICE.md` — intended for contributors, writers, and collaborators who aren't working through the agent system.

---

## Who We're Writing For

The average AXIS reader has never read an astrology article before. They found us because something in the world felt off and they wanted a different lens on it. Our job is to give them that lens without making them feel like they're missing a degree to understand us.

Write like you're explaining something fascinating to a smart friend at dinner — not like you're presenting at a conference.

---

## The One Rule

**If someone's grandmother couldn't follow the first sentence, rewrite it.**

This doesn't mean dumbing it down. It means earning the depth. Hook them with something real and human first. Then go as deep as the subject demands.

---

## Post Structure That Works

**1. Open with what's happening in the world.**
Start with a real event, a feeling in the cultural air, something the reader already recognizes. Then connect it to what's in the sky.

**2. Name the planet or transit in plain English.**
"Saturn is moving through the part of the sky associated with collective systems and accountability" — not "Saturn transits Aquarius."

**3. Explain what this tends to surface.**
Not what will happen. What patterns tend to emerge. Keep it grounded in observable human behavior and historical precedent.

**4. Connect to a tradition.**
Briefly name which ancestral or cosmological framework informs this read and why it's relevant. One or two sentences. No lectures.

**5. Close with something actionable or reflective.**
Give the reader something to sit with or do. A question. A reframe. A thing to watch for in their own life or in the news.

## Terms That Need Translation (Every Time, First Use)

| Term | Plain-English Translation |
|---|---|
| Sidereal | Based on the actual position of stars, not the simplified Western calendar system |
| Nakshatra | One of 27 lunar mansions — smaller segments of the sky that add detail to a planet's position |
| Rahu / Ketu | The north and south nodes — points where the moon's path crosses the sun's path; associated with collective destiny and release |
| Ayanamsa | The degree of correction applied to align calculations with the actual sky |
| Sub-lord | A finer layer of planetary influence within a nakshatra (KP methodology) |
| Dasha | A planetary time period — a cycle of years ruled by a particular planet |
| Mundane astrology | Sky analysis applied to world events, not individual charts |

---

## Tone Reference

- **Do:** Direct, warm, grounded, precise, respectful of the reader's intelligence
- **Don't:** Vague, mystical-sounding, prediction-heavy, jargon-first, defensive about the methodology

---

## Before You Publish Checklist

- [ ] First sentence passes the grandmother test
- [ ] Every technical term translated on first use
- [ ] Sky connected to real-world event or pattern
- [ ] Ancestral tradition named and contextualized (not just dropped)
- [ ] No hard predictions — reads and patterns only
- [ ] CTA or closing reflection included
