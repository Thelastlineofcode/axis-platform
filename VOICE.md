## The Core Rule

**Write for someone who has never read an astrology article.**

Everything on AstroloG — homepage copy, about page, post titles, widget labels, newsletter subject lines, CTA buttons — must be understandable to a general adult with zero astrology background. If a term requires insider knowledge to parse, it must be followed immediately by a plain-English explanation.

AstroloG speaks in the modern voice of a young New Orleans native — clear, lyrical, authoritative. Blend logic and mysticism. Avoid fear or fatalism. Write like a sage-scientist with reverence and rhythm.

---

## Audience

The AstroloG reader is:
- A young Black or Brown person encountering ancestral cosmology for the first time
- Curious about what's happening in the world and why their body, lineage, or environment feels connected to larger forces
- Open to African/Indigenous frameworks — Dogon, Ifá, Vodou, Khemetic — as legitimate science, not folklore
- **Not** necessarily a practicing astrologer or Western horoscope reader
- Likely arriving from social media, a friend's share, or a community post — not an astrology search
- Smart, skeptical of vague mystical content, and hungry for precision

Write to them as a knowledgeable elder cousin who knows the sky deeply — not as a teacher lecturing students.

## Voice Principles

**1. Lead with outcome, not methodology.**
Don't say what system you're using before you say what the reader gets.
- ❌ "Using Lahiri ayanamsa sidereal positioning, Mars enters Capricorn..."
- ✅ "Mars is moving into the part of the sky associated with discipline and long-game strategy. Here's what that means for the next few weeks."

**2. Translate every technical term the first time it appears.**
Jargon is allowed — it adds credibility and depth. Never let it land without a translation.
- ❌ "Ketu in Virgo is intensifying the 6th house theme."
- ✅ "Ketu (the south node — the point in the sky associated with release and karmic patterns) is in Virgo right now, which tends to surface themes around health, daily routine, and what we need to let go of."

**3. Connect sky to Earth every time.**
Every planetary position must be anchored to something happening in the real world — an event, a cultural moment, an economic pattern, a political shift, a body, a bloodline. The sky is the lens, not the story.

**4. No predictions. Real reads.**
AstroloG does not predict futures. It reads the present moment using the sky as a map.
- ❌ "This will be a difficult month for finances."
- ✅ "The current sky tends to surface tension around resources and value — here's what to watch for."

**5. Ancestral traditions are named and honored, not performed.**
Dogon, Ifá, Vodou, and Khemetic cosmology are frameworks that inform the analysis. Name them. Explain briefly why they matter. Don't use them as aesthetic decoration.
- ❌ "In the Ifá tradition... [unexplained drop]"
- ✅ "Ifá — the Yoruba divination system with thousands of years of sky observation behind it — tracks this kind of transit as..."

**6. The Blood Remembers standard.**
When writing about environmental harm, ancestral illness, lineage patterns, or political injustice — lead with the human story. The data and the sky layer follow. Never lead with statistics. Lead with a name, a date, a body.

## UI Copy Rules

| Context | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Section headers | Plain noun phrases, no jargon | "Where the Planets Are Today" not "Sidereal Transits (Lahiri)" |
| CTA buttons | Action-first, warm | "I'm In", "Read This Week's Issue", "Get the Free Read" |
| Widget labels | Descriptive, not technical | "What this means" not "Sub-Lord" |
| Empty states | Human, anticipatory | "First issue dropping soon. Don't miss it." |
| Footer/footnotes | Honest, brief | "Calculated using actual sky positions" not "Lahiri Ayanamsa" |

---

## What's Allowed in Body Copy (Articles)

Once a reader has chosen to read a full article, the depth bar rises. You may use:
- Technical nakshatra names — with a one-line explanation on first use
- Ayanamsa references — in a footnote or parenthetical
- Sub-lord methodology — explained in context
- Ancestral tradition frameworks — named and briefly contextualized
- Medical and scientific citations — inline, not in footnotes
- Political and legal record — named, dated, sourced

The rule in articles: **earn the jargon**. Get the reader invested in the story first, then go deep.
